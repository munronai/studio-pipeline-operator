# outreach-templates.md — Paste-Ready Artifacts

The drafted things. Lead with the prospect's problem, not the service. A specific observation beats a pitch. Two specific days beats "whenever works." Fill every bracket with something real before it ships — a generic `[specific observation]` is worse than no email.

---

## Cold-call angles (pick ONE per call)

### Angle 1 — The specific observation (DEFAULT)
Do 5 min of research; find one broken/missing thing on their site; lead with it.
```
"Hey [Name], this is Jayden — quick question before you hang up.
I was looking at [Business Name]'s site before calling and noticed
[specific thing: your contact form returns an error / you don't have a
booking button / the site isn't loading on mobile / your hours aren't
listed anywhere]. That something you've been meaning to fix?"
```

### Angle 2 — The AI / speed angle (when Angle 1 isn't obvious)
Their site is fine but you still want to call.
```
"Hey [Name], this is Jayden — I run a small AI-augmented studio.
We build websites in 5-7 days — what normally takes an agency
6-8 weeks. Got 60 seconds?"
```

### Angle 3 — The AI receptionist pivot (for service businesses)
Contractors, plumbers, HVAC, salons, dental — anywhere missed calls = missed revenue.
```
"Hey [Name], this is Jayden — I build AI phone systems for local
businesses. Basically an AI that answers your calls 24/7, qualifies
leads, and books appointments when you're on a job or after hours.
Do you miss calls when you're busy?"
```

## The 60-second qualifier (once they engage — 3 questions, in order)

**Q1 — Is the problem real?**
```
"Is [the website / missing calls / whatever opened the conversation]
actually costing you customers right now, or is it more of a
'nice to fix someday' thing?"
```
**Q2 — Are you the decision-maker?**
```
"Is this something you'd decide on your own, or is there a partner
or someone else involved?"
```
**Q3 — Is there a budget?** (don't ask "what's your budget")
```
"We work in a range — smaller projects around $2,500-3,500, bigger
builds $5,000-12,500. Does that range work for what you're thinking?"
```

---

## The booking close (the two-days ask)
If Q1–Q3 are green, close for a 15-minute call — not a proposal.
```
"This sounds like something I might actually be able to help with.
I do a short 15-minute call first — just to see if there's a real
fit before either of us wastes time. Are you free this week,
[Tuesday or Wednesday]?"
```
Give two specific days, they pick one. If they want info first:
```
"Totally. I'll text you my website so you can take a look — if it
feels right, just text me back and we'll find a time. Fair?"
```

## Cold email — Template 1 (specific-observation opener, DEFAULT)
**Subject:** Quick question about [Business Name]
```
Hi [First Name],

I came across [Business Name] while looking at [category] in [city].
[One specific observation that shows you actually looked at their site —
not "I love your brand" but something concrete: "your menu page is
loading the PDF on mobile" or "I noticed bookings are phone-only" or
"your contact form has been broken for at least a month."]

I run a small AI-augmented studio called GriffainAI. We build [websites,
software, etc.] for small businesses — typically 1-2 weeks turnaround,
pricing usually $2,500-12,500 depending on scope.

If a [specific service that addresses their gap] would help, I'd love
to talk. Otherwise no worries — just wanted to put it on your radar.

Either way, you can see some of my work at jaydenforshee.com.

Jayden Forshee
GriffainAI
[phone number]
```
Under 150 words. First-person. Specific over vague.

## Follow-up sequence (max 2 touches on a cold email, then stop)

**Follow-up 1 — 5–7 days after initial send**
**Subject:** Re: [original subject line]
```
Hi [First Name],

Following up on the email below. If now's not the right time, no problem —
just wanted to make sure it didn't get buried.

Jayden
```
Quote the original below the reply.

**Follow-up 2 — 10–14 days after Follow-up 1 (FINAL)**
**Subject:** Closing the loop — [original subject]
```
Hi [First Name],

Closing the loop on my note from a couple weeks ago. I'll stop reaching
out — if you want to talk in the future, my email and number are below.

Jayden
GriffainAI
[phone number]
jaydenforshee.com
```
No more after FU2. "I'll stop reaching out" reduces guilt and sometimes triggers a reply.

> **Proposal-stage cadence** (Station 5): FU1 at +5 days ("did this get buried"), FU2 at +7 more days ("closing the loop"), then `stage: lost`, reason `ghosted`. Two follow-ups, then the pipeline moves on.

## Warm-network message (launch announcement — hand-sent, never BCC)
**Subject:** Quick update — new project
```
Hey [Name],

It's Jayden. Hope you're doing well — wanted to share something.

I've launched GriffainAI, a small AI-augmented digital studio. We build
websites, custom software, AI systems, and the rest of the digital
stack for small businesses. The pitch is: agency-quality work delivered
3-5x faster because of how I'm leveraging AI.

I'm in the launch phase, taking my first clients. If you know anyone
who needs this kind of help — or you yourself are thinking about
anything along these lines — I'd love to talk.

Either way, hope things are good. Let's catch up sometime.

Jayden
```
One launch message + maybe one post-first-engagement update + quarterly check-ins. Past that you're spamming people you care about.

## Decline (verbatim template)
```
Hey [Name],

Thanks for the call yesterday. After thinking about it more, I don't think
GriffainAI is the right fit for [the project / what you're trying to do].

[Specific reason, brief, not accusatory. e.g., "Your timeline is more
compressed than what we can deliver at our quality bar." OR "Your needs are
more specialized than our current capabilities cover." OR "What you're
describing is closer to vendor work than the studio work we do."]

[Optional referral: "Have you tried [resource]? They might be a better fit
for this kind of project."]

Best of luck with [the work / the business].

Jayden
```

## Referral (the routes)
- **Sub-$750** → *"At that budget I'd actually point you to a template builder like Squarespace or a freelancer on Fiverr; you'll get something live without overpaying for studio work. If your needs grow down the line, my door's open."*
- **$25K+ enterprise** → mention Substrate as the likely home; escalate + flag for the human, don't decline.
- **Off-lane** (video/photo/print/standalone-SEO/ad-ops) → an industry-specific specialist or Upwork.

Three decline rules: don't ghost · don't over-apologize · don't burn the bridge.
