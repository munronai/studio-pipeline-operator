# qualifier-protocol.md — The 15-Minute Fit Call

The qualifier is a **filter, not a sales call.** Its job is to lose the wrong leads fast — 70–80% of inquiries reach it, only 20–30% pass it. A qualifier that advances everyone is broken. Never pitch, never quote a specific price.

Output: **proceed / decline / refer** + the drafted next artifact.

---

## The 5 core questions (verbatim, in order)

### 1. What problem are you trying to solve?
Open here. Let them describe it in their own words. **Don't lead. Don't pitch. Listen.**
*Listen for:* clarity vs. confusion · the specific business pain · symptom vs. underlying issue · urgency markers. Clear in 30–90s = they've done diagnostic work on themselves (easier engagement). Rambles 3+ min = needs the full diagnostic to find what they need (still qualifiable).

### 2. What does your business actually do?
The substrate question, scaled to 15 min. What they say is rarely the full picture.
*Listen for:* real revenue source vs. what they think the business is about · who actually pays them · what the company delivers day to day. Note ontology gaps; don't dive in — that's diagnostic territory.

### 3. What's your budget range?
The most disqualifying question. Most balk here — that's the filter working.
Framing: *"Our work ranges from about $750 for quick deliverables to $12,500 for two-week complex builds. Where in that range were you thinking?"*

### 4. When do you need this done by?
Timeline-fit. Reveals real deadline vs. vague wanting and whether tier timelines fit.

### 5. What have you tried before?
Reveals failure modes, vendor history, what's already ruled out. Tried-3-vendors-and-disappointed = either impossible to please or had the wrong vendors (the diagnostic decides which). Tried nothing = first-time buyer, needs more education.

## Budget branch (decision points, verbatim)

| They say | Disposition |
|---|---|
| Balks at $750 | **Not a fit, decline politely.** |
| "$500 max" | **Not a fit, refer** to Fiverr or template solutions. |
| "$2K–5K" | **Standard tier candidate, proceed.** |
| "$5K–15K" | **Build tier candidate, proceed.** |
| "$25K+" | **Potentially a Substrate engagement** — mention but proceed with diagnostic (escalate + flag; don't decline the whale). |
| "Whatever it takes" | **Push for specifics. A vague budget always means a low budget.** If they won't band it, treat as below floor. |

---

## Timeline branch (decision points, verbatim)

| They say | Disposition |
|---|---|
| "Need it by tomorrow" | **Decline unless Express tier fits exactly** (1–3 days, contained scope). |
| "Within a week" | Standard tier candidate. |
| "Within a month" | Standard or Build candidate, comfortable. |
| "By [specific date 2+ weeks out]" | Ask why that date — reveals the real deadline. |
| "Whenever" | **Red flag.** Vague timeline usually means no real commitment. |

## The decision after 15 minutes

**PROCEED → diagnostic** (yes path)
Clear problem + real urgency + viable budget (≥ floor) + reasonable timeline + no hard flag. Even if some elements are unclear, the gestalt is right. → Draft the **diagnostic-confirm email**: schedule within **3–5 days**, say what to bring. Move `qualifier → diagnostic`.

**DECLINE** (no path)
Budget below floor · timeline impossible · wrong scope · wrong fit (industry/ethics) · buyer dynamics off (vendor labor). Or any **hard walk-away flag** (`walk-away-flags.md`). → Draft the warm decline, `stage: lost` with the matching reason.

**REFER** (referral path)
Legitimate need, just not a studio match. → Draft the referral: **sub-$750 → Fiverr/Upwork**; **$25K+ enterprise → mention Substrate**; **off-lane → industry specialist**; maintenance-only → ongoing-support vendor. `stage: lost`, reason `referred-elsewhere`.

---

## Never do in the qualifier
- Never pitch the services — it's fit-finding, not selling.
- Never quote a specific price — tier ranges only; numbers come after the diagnostic.
- Never agree to scope creep — "Let's discuss in the full diagnostic."
- Never make timeline commitments — tier timelines only.
- Never over-explain the studio — they already agreed to the call.

---

## Conversion math (the qualifier is the choke point)
- 70–80% of inquiries become qualifier calls.
- 20–30% of qualifier calls proceed to full diagnostic.
- 50–70% of full diagnostics convert to signed engagements.
- End-to-end: ~10–15% of inquiries become paying clients.

Higher conversion usually = under-pricing/under-scoping. Lower = weak lead qualification upstream.
