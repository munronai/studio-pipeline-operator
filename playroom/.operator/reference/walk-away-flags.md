# walk-away-flags.md — Flags, Lean-Ins, and the Decline

A hard flag **stops the line**. You decline or refer; you do not advance. A bad engagement is more expensive than no engagement. A lean-in signal does the opposite — it overrides the grade upward. The decline itself is an asset: a clean warm decline preserves referral karma and a future re-entry.

Target decline rate: **50–70% of inquiries.** Most leads are not a fit; the discipline of saying no is what protects the studio.

---

## The 7 hard walk-away flags (verbatim keys + labels)

Any one = decline or refer. Stop advancing.

| Key | Label | Trips when |
|---|---|---|
| `budget-below-floor` | Budget below $750 floor | Stated/implied budget under $750. Refer, never negotiate the floor. |
| `timeline-impossible` | Timeline impossible for quality | "Need it in 24h" for anything beyond a clean Express deliverable. |
| `wrong-scope` | Wants a service we don't offer | Primary need is video / photo / print / email-platform / standalone SEO / ad-ops. |
| `excluded-industry` | Excluded industry | Gambling, crypto/NFT, MLM, high-pressure sales, adult, predatory lending, illegal. |
| `vendor-labor` | Vendor-labor dynamics (just-build-it) | "Just give me a quote, I don't need to talk." "Just build what I asked." Balks at the diagnostic. Rejects the prescription for the original ask. |
| `no-decision-maker` | Can't reach the decision-maker | Not the DM and the DM won't take a call; multiple DMs no lead; "I'll check with my [partner/committee] on every detail." |
| `pricing-pressure` | Pricing pressure / haggling | "Can you discount?" before the proposal. "Other agencies are cheaper." Negotiating before scope is set. Pay-after-delivery. |

Map the flag to its `decline_reason` in `pipeline-fields.md` when you write `stage: lost`.

## Soft walk-aways (decline with care, or refer)

Not auto-kills — judgment calls. Refer with a resource when possible to keep the relationship.

**1. Genuine non-fit, no fault** — need too specialized for our generalist phase · category we're avoiding while building portfolio · too sophisticated for our pricing (premium-agency territory) · too unsophisticated for our process (would benefit from a template).

**2. Personality mismatch** — rude during qualifier/diagnostic · denigrates previous vendors (they'll do it to you) · entitlement/controlling/condescension · chronically late or disorganized. *Trust the instinct. A bad engagement is more expensive than no engagement.*

**3. Reality-disconnected expectations** — "a site like Apple's for $500" · "convert at 30%+" · "millions of visitors month one" · beliefs that contradict basic mechanics. Educate briefly; if they don't update, decline.

## Lean-in signals (override the grade up, prioritize, say why)

The inverse of walk-aways. When these appear, move fast — even on a B/C lead.

**1. Strategic value beyond fee** — a category we want in the case-study library · well-connected, likely to refer · teaches us something new · a halo client whose name lifts our credibility.

**2. Excellent fit signals** — real thinking in their initial message · read our material, asked informed questions · clear problem + matching budget · respects the diagnostic process.

**3. Personality fit** — direct, clear communication · respect for the studio's process · excitement to work together · decision-making capacity (they decide and move).

When a lean-in surfaces: state the grade, then state the override and the reason. *"B (70) by the rubric, but they read the portfolio and asked an informed question + run in a referral-rich circle — prioritize as if A."*

## The decline script (verbatim template)

```
Hey [Name],

Thanks for the call yesterday. After thinking about it more, I don't think
GriffainAI is the right fit for [the project / what you're trying to do].

[Specific reason, brief, not accusatory. e.g., "Your timeline is more
compressed than what we can deliver at our quality bar." OR "Your needs are
more specialized than our current capabilities cover." OR "What you're
describing is closer to vendor work than the studio work we do."]

[Optional referral: "Have you tried [resource]? They might be a better fit
for this kind of project."]

Best of luck with [the work / the business].

Jayden
```

**Three rules for declines:**
1. Don't ghost. Always send a decline message.
2. Don't apologize excessively. Brief, honest, no over-explanation.
3. Don't burn bridges. Maintain the relationship; you may want them as a referral source later.

**Re-entry vs dead:** lost to *timing* or *a budget that may grow* → 60–90 day nurture re-entry. Lost to *fit, vendor-mode, or studio-declined* → archive, no re-entry.
