# gate-checklist.md — The Hard Auto-Disqualify Gate

Run this **before scoring**. Any one box checked = **KILL** with the gate reason. No score, no draft. Most leads die here, and that is the point — gating protects credit, attention, and Claude effort.

Disposition for a gated lead: `stage: lost` (or no advance), the gate reason stated, **no artifact**.

---

## The checklist (run top to bottom; first hit kills)

- [ ] **No phone number.** Can't reach a decision-maker → no pipeline value. KILL.
- [ ] **Business closed / not operational.** Permanently closed, defunct, no signs of life. KILL.
- [ ] **National chain or franchise.** Can't touch the website, no local decision-maker. KILL. *(A whale that is multi-location but founder-owned is NOT this — that escalates, see rules.md.)*
- [ ] **Retail / food (not a service business).** Pure retail or food-service with no service-business need. KILL. *(Restaurants that need a site can be a Pattern-A target — judge by whether the studio's services fit; a generic storefront with no web need is a kill.)*
- [ ] **Excluded industry** (see list below). KILL.
- [ ] **Primary need is a service we don't offer** (see list below). KILL or refer.

If all six are clear → proceed to `scoring-rubric.md`.

---

## Excluded industries (verbatim — won't serve)

- Gambling
- Cryptocurrency / NFT speculation projects
- Multi-level marketing
- High-pressure sales operations
- Adult content / explicit material
- Predatory lending / payday loans
- Anything illegal or near-illegal

Decline politely, no detailed explanation. The studio has the right to choose its clients.

---

## Services we don't offer (verbatim — refer, don't admit)

If the lead's **primary** need is one of these, it's out of scope:

- Video production
- Photography (we use stock or the client's existing assets)
- Print design (business cards, flyers, brochures)
- Email marketing setup (we do strategy and content; not platform configuration)
- SEO campaigns as a standalone service
- Paid ad management as a primary service (we do strategy; ad operations is contractor work)

If a lead's primary need is one of these → refer elsewhere, don't draft studio outreach.

---

## What this gate is NOT

- It is **not** a quality judgment — a great-fit business with a missing phone still dies here (reachability is real).
- It does **not** apply to size. Never kill a lead for being "small." Decline on fit, not size. A small business that fits the process beats a big one that doesn't.
- A $25K+ multi-service founder-owned lead is **not** a chain kill — it escalates to the diagnostic and gets flagged (the whale rule). Read `rules.md` edge cases before killing anything large.
