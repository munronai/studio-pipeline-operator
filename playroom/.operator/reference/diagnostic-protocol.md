# diagnostic-protocol.md — The 60-Minute Diagnostic

Only happens with qualified prospects. Output: enough understanding to write a tight proposal within 24 hours. **The diagnostic is the work, not the prelude to the work.** When you draft the diagnostic-confirm, tell them what to bring and set the 24-hour-proposal expectation.

Structure: ~60 minutes across six blocks.

---

## Opening (5 min)
- Confirm timing + agenda.
- Acknowledge one thing from the qualifier (don't dwell).
- Set expectations: *"This call is for me to understand your situation in enough depth that I can write you a real proposal within 24 hours. I'll ask a lot of questions. Some will feel basic — they're not."*
- Then **let them talk**: *"Tell me what you do, as if you were describing it to a friend at dinner."*

## Substrate exploration (15 min) — the heart of it
*Listen for:* what they say vs. what their materials say · where language gets specific vs. vague · what they downplay/apologize for (often important) · what they get animated about (often the real core).

Key questions:
- What does your business actually do, day to day?
- Do you describe it the same way to people inside vs. outside your industry?
- What's the gap between what your website says and what you actually do?
- Walk me through your typical customer — who are they when they arrive, what are they when they leave?
- What part of the business is most exhausting? What part feels easy?
- If you could clone yourself onto one part of the business, which part?

→ The substrate finding (what the business actually *is* vs. what's stated) frames the proposal's "what I heard" opening.

## The problem in their own words (10 min)
- Of all your problems right now, which one made you reach out?
- What would success look like 90 days after we finish?
- What have you already tried for this, and why didn't it work?
- A year from now, everything went perfectly — what's different?
- What's the cost of not solving this? (revenue, time, sanity, opportunity)

*Listen for:* whether the stated problem matches the substrate gap · whether they've diagnosed themselves correctly · real vs. vague urgency.

## Audience and customer signal (10 min)
- Walk me through your three most recent customers — who, how they found you, what they bought.
- When customers describe you to friends, what do they say?
- Most common reason someone says "this isn't for me"?
- What customer do you wish you had more of? Less of?

*Listen for:* gap between target and actual customer · customer language vs. company language · where retention happens or doesn't.

## Voice and brand calibration (10 min)
- Show me 3–5 sites in your industry that do this well — what you like about each.
- Show me 3–5 you hate — why.
- If your business had a voice — formal, casual, warm, sharp, professional — what is it?
- Read me a paragraph of your About page. Sound like how you talk in person?
- What words do you use that nobody in your industry uses? What words do they use that you avoid?

→ Map preferences to an aesthetic vocabulary; capture primary + modifiers; note brand elements to preserve (logo, color, photography).

## Logistics close (5 min)
- Timeline — what's driving the date?
- Budget — within the qualifier range, what's your real ceiling?
- Who else needs to approve this on your side?
- What's our next step from here?

Set the proposal expectation: *"I'll send a written proposal within 24 hours — what I heard, what I'd recommend, timeline, price, what we'd need from each other. One revision conversation if you want changes. Then sign and we kick off."*

---

## Listen underneath the answers
Decision-maker presence · sales resistance (buyer-style "what do I get / how much" vs. partner-style "help me figure out what I need") · quality bar (sophisticated vs. vibing) · urgency authenticity (real = fast yes; vague = slow no) · personality fit (could you work with them for 1–4 weeks?).

## Decline mid-diagnostic if it surfaces
They want vendor labor not studio work · a competing vendor relationship · pricing pressure putting them below floor · values/culture misfit. → End gracefully, send a follow-up, decline politely with a referral. Don't burn 24 hours on a proposal you won't honor.

---

## After the call (route the findings)
Within 2h: notes with verbatim quotes; map to aesthetic; map to **service pattern A–H** (`service-routing.md`); determine **tier** (Express/Standard/Build/Retainer); determine **specific price** (pricing decision tree). Within 24h: write + send the proposal (problem recap → prescription → why → deliverables → timeline → price → next step). Move `diagnostic → proposal`.
